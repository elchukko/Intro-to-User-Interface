    Why Should I Get a Certification?

    Certifications are worth the investment. They give you an edge on the competition in a job search or help you move up within the ranks in your current organization.

    The CompTIA testing organization notes that "In Q3 2014, IT positions made up 11% of all job openings, and IT certifications can help get you in the door. An overwhelming majority of IT professionals and their hiring managers agree on the value of certifications. 93% of hiring managers believe certifications are beneficial."

    Businesses want certifications because it shows definitively that the talent they hire can perform at a certain level.

    Furthermore, research shows that the learning curve is much shorter for a certified IT worker than it is for a noncertified counterpart. Certified workers are more likely to fix problems right the first time and are more adept at anticipating issues and resolving them before they become major problems.

    Certifications Can Mean Better Soft Skills

    A Certified IT pros tend to have better soft skills including better communication and project management skills. Being able to translate business talk into tech talk and back again; or combining tech skills with business savvy, such as project management, is a major differentiator for the IT professional.

    Three General Classifications of Certifications

    Federal or State Issued

    • Certifications like teaching, legal, social work, and medical.
    • Certifications are required to perform this work.
    • In the field of social work, it's often who pays the bill will determine whether you can work.
    • Example: Medicaid requires practitioners have a certain level of certification to be paid by Medicaid. Prectitioners can see patients that selfpay with a lower level of certification. If you want to be hired by an agency that handles Medicaid patients, you must get the certification required by Medicaid.

    Industry Certifications

    • Industry-sanctioned groups issue these certifications.
    • You may see job descriptions that say "PMP certification preferred."
    • Example 1: The PMP certification is issued by the Product Management Institute (PMI).
    • Example 2: SSHRM (Society of Human Resources Management) issues a variety of certifications like PHR or PSHR.

    Corporate Certification

    • Corporation issue these certifications.
    • Most are technical in nature, like CCNA from Cisco or MCP from Microsoft.
    • They can be time consuming and expensice to attain and maintain.
